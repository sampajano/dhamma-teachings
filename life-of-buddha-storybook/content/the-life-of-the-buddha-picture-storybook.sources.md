# The Life of the Buddha - Story Source Notes

This file tracks likely textual sources for each scene in the picture storybook. It is not a critical edition; it is a practical source map for future editing.

Classification:

- Direct sutta: the main story is substantially found in a Pali sutta.
- Partial sutta: the doctrinal or narrative core is in a sutta, while important story details come from later biography or commentary.
- Vinaya: the main story is preserved in the Pali Vinaya rather than a sutta.
- Commentary or later tradition: the story is mainly known from commentaries, Jataka introductory material, later biographies, art-historical tradition, or modern retellings.
- Composite: the scene is a broad synthesis rather than a single-source story.

### Confidence After Verification

Overall confidence in the broad classifications is about 95%. I would not claim 99%, because several scenes belong to layered Buddha-biography tradition where the same motif appears in multiple later sources, and the exact earliest recoverable source is not always easy to pin down.

High-confidence groups:

- Direct sutta scenes: about 98-99% confident.
- Vinaya scenes: about 95-98% confident.
- Jataka Nidana-katha early-life scenes: about 95% confident after checking the Jataka Introduction material for Maya's dream, Lumbini birth, four signs, palace dancers, silent farewell, and the Great Renunciation.

Lower-confidence or more nuanced entries:

- Scene 08, the wounded swan: about 75% confident as "later retelling"; the story is widely circulated, but I have not verified a strong early Pali canonical or commentarial source.
- Scenes 35-36, Tavatimsa teaching and descent: about 90% confident for the Dhammapada Commentary / Devorohana Vatthu tradition; lower for specific visual details such as exact stairway materials, which vary across artistic and later narrative traditions.
- Scene 38, Jivaka treating the Buddha's foot: about 99% confident for the Buddha's foot being wounded by a stone sliver, because this is supported by SN 1.38 and Vinaya/Devadatta traditions; about 90% confident for Jivaka's treatment details, which are mainly commentarial and dictionary tradition rather than the sutta itself.
- Scene 32, Yasodhara meeting the Buddha again: about 90-95% confident as Jataka Nidana-katha / later biography rather than direct sutta.

## Detailed Notes

## Scene 01 - The Great Aspiration Before Dipankara Buddha

Classification: Commentary or later tradition, with canonical Khuddaka support.

Likely sources:

- Buddhavamsa, especially the Dipankara Buddha account.
- Jataka Nidana-katha, the introductory life story attached to the Jataka commentary.
- Later Theravada Buddha-life retellings.

Notes: The Sumedha-Dipankara aspiration is not a Nikaya sutta story. Buddhavamsa is canonical in the Theravada Khuddaka Nikaya, but it is a later devotional/biographical text rather than an early discourse.

## Scene 02 - The Hair Spread in the Mud and the Prophecy of Future Buddhahood

Classification: Commentary or later tradition, with canonical Khuddaka support.

Likely sources:

- Buddhavamsa, Dipankara Buddha chapter.
- Jataka Nidana-katha.
- Later Buddha-life traditions and Buddhist art.

Notes: The core details of Sumedha lying in the mud, spreading his hair, and receiving Dipankara Buddha's prediction belong to the Sumedha-Dipankara cycle, not to a Nikaya sutta.

## Scene 03 - Queen Maya's White Elephant Dream

Classification: Commentary or later tradition.

Likely sources:

- Jataka Nidana-katha.
- Later Buddha biographies such as the Lalitavistara and Buddhacarita traditions.

Notes: MN 123, Acchariya-abbhuta Sutta, gives canonical material about the Bodhisatta's descent from Tusita and conception, but the white elephant dream itself is a later biographical motif rather than a direct sutta episode.

## Scene 04 - Queen Maya in Lumbini Grove

Classification: Partial sutta.

Likely sources:

- MN 123, Acchariya-abbhuta Sutta, for the Bodhisatta's birth standing up and associated marvels.
- Jataka Nidana-katha and later Buddha biographies for the Lumbini grove setting, travel to the maternal home, and tree-branch birth scene.

Notes: The story is partly anchored in sutta material, but the fully developed Lumbini narrative is primarily biographical tradition.

## Scene 05 - The Birth of Prince Siddhartha

Classification: Direct sutta, with traditional embellishment.

Likely sources:

- MN 123, Acchariya-abbhuta Sutta.
- Parallel birth narratives in later Buddha-life traditions.

Notes: The seven steps, white parasol, and final-birth proclamation are directly found in MN 123. The surrounding courtly and devotional scene is a later narrative framing.

## Scene 06 - The Sage Asita's Prediction

Classification: Direct sutta.

Likely sources:

- Snp 3.11, Nalaka Sutta.
- Later Buddha-life retellings.

Notes: Snp 3.11 preserves the Asita/Nalaka material in canonical sutta form, including Asita's joy and sorrow over not living long enough to hear the future Buddha's teaching.

## Scene 07 - The Young Prince Meditates Beneath the Tree

Classification: Partial sutta.

Likely sources:

- MN 36, Mahasaccaka Sutta, for the Bodhisatta remembering the first jhana under the rose-apple tree while his father was working.
- Later biographical traditions for the plowing festival setting and the expanded compassion scene involving disturbed insects and field life.

Notes: The first-jhana memory is directly sutta-based. The childhood episode as a detailed public festival scene is later narrative development.

## Scene 08 - Siddhartha Saves the Wounded Swan

Classification: Commentary or later tradition.

Likely sources:

- Later Buddha-life retellings and modern children's biographies.
- Possibly Sanskrit/North Indian narrative traditions, but not a clear Pali sutta source.

Notes: This is a widely loved Siddhartha childhood story, but it is not found as a direct episode in the Pali suttas. The exact textual origin remains unresolved here.

## Scene 09 - The Prince's Skill and Strength

Classification: Commentary or later tradition.

Likely sources:

- Jataka Nidana-katha.
- Lalitavistara, Buddhacarita, and other later Buddha biographies.

Notes: The general idea of Siddhartha's princely upbringing is traditional. Details about competitions, arts, weapons, and extraordinary strength are mostly developed in later biographies rather than early suttas.

## Scene 10 - Siddhartha and Yasodhara Are Married

Classification: Commentary or later tradition.

Likely sources:

- Jataka Nidana-katha.
- Later Buddha-life biographies.

Notes: The suttas refer to the Buddha's son Rahula and family background, but the marriage story as a scene belongs mainly to biographical tradition.

## Scene 11 - The Four Outings and the Four Sights

Classification: Partial sutta / later biography.

Likely sources:

- DN 14, Mahapadana Sutta, for the four sights pattern in the life of Vipassi Buddha.
- Jataka Nidana-katha and later Buddha biographies for applying the four sights narrative to Gotama Buddha.
- MN 26, Ariyapariyesana Sutta, for Gotama's existential reflection on aging, sickness, death, and the noble search.

Notes: The fully formed four outings story for Siddhartha is not directly narrated in the early suttas. The pattern is canonical for Vipassi Buddha and later becomes standard in Gotama's biography.

## Scene 12 - The Palace Pleasures Lose Their Charm

Classification: Commentary or later tradition.

Likely sources:

- Jataka Nidana-katha.
- Later Buddha biographies.

Notes: The image of sleeping dancers and palace pleasures becoming unattractive is a classic Buddha-life motif, but it is not a direct Nikaya sutta episode.

## Scene 13 - The Silent Farewell

Classification: Commentary or later tradition.

Likely sources:

- Jataka Nidana-katha.
- Later Buddha biographies and devotional retellings.

Notes: The suttas preserve the fact of renunciation, but the quiet farewell to Yasodhara and Rahula is later narrative elaboration.

## Scene 14 - The Great Renunciation

Classification: Partial sutta.

Likely sources:

- MN 26, Ariyapariyesana Sutta, for leaving home while young, with parents grieving.
- MN 36, Mahasaccaka Sutta, for the beginning of the Bodhisatta's search.
- Jataka Nidana-katha for Channa, Kanthaka, the night departure, and dramatic palace details.

Notes: Renunciation is strongly sutta-based, but the full dramatic Great Renunciation scene is later biography.

## Scene 15 - Cutting the Hair and Leaving Royal Signs Behind

Classification: Partial sutta.

Likely sources:

- MN 26, Ariyapariyesana Sutta, for shaving hair and beard and putting on ochre robes.
- Jataka Nidana-katha and later Buddha biographies for the heavenly reception of the topknot and more detailed symbolism.

Notes: The core act of going forth is directly sutta-based. The relic/topknot scene is later tradition.

## Scene 16 - Learning from Alara Kalama

Classification: Direct sutta.

Likely sources:

- MN 26, Ariyapariyesana Sutta.
- MN 36, Mahasaccaka Sutta, with related autobiographical search material.

Notes: The Bodhisatta's training under Alara Kalama and attainment of the base of nothingness is directly sutta-based.

## Scene 17 - Leaving Alara Kalama

Classification: Direct sutta.

Likely sources:

- MN 26, Ariyapariyesana Sutta.

Notes: The invitation to remain as an equal and the Bodhisatta's decision that this attainment did not lead to final liberation are directly sutta-based.

## Scene 18 - Learning from Uddaka Ramaputta

Classification: Direct sutta.

Likely sources:

- MN 26, Ariyapariyesana Sutta.
- MN 36, Mahasaccaka Sutta, with related autobiographical search material.

Notes: The Bodhisatta's training in the base of neither-perception-nor-non-perception is directly sutta-based.

## Scene 19 - Leaving Uddaka Ramaputta

Classification: Direct sutta.

Likely sources:

- MN 26, Ariyapariyesana Sutta.

Notes: The Bodhisatta's refusal to mistake refined meditative attainment for final liberation is directly sutta-based.

## Scene 20 - Practicing with the Five Ascetics

Classification: Partial sutta.

Likely sources:

- MN 36, Mahasaccaka Sutta, for severe austerities.
- Vinaya Mahavagga, Mahakhandhaka, for early narrative around the group of five.
- Later biography for some named and dramatic details.

Notes: The five ascetics are directly connected with the first teaching tradition, and severe ascetic practice is strongly sutta-based. This particular scene is a synthesis.

## Scene 21 - The Limits of Austerity

Classification: Direct sutta.

Likely sources:

- MN 36, Mahasaccaka Sutta.
- Related material in MN 85 and MN 100.

Notes: The extreme austerities, the bodily descriptions, the reflection that this did not lead to awakening, and the memory of the first jhana are directly sutta-based.

## Scene 22 - Sujata's Offering of Milk-Rice

Classification: Partial sutta / later biography.

Likely sources:

- MN 36, Mahasaccaka Sutta, for taking solid food and the five ascetics leaving.
- Jataka Nidana-katha for Sujata, the milk-rice, and the detailed offering scene.

Notes: Accepting nourishment and being abandoned by the five ascetics are sutta-based. Sujata by name and the devotional milk-rice episode are later biographical tradition.

## Scene 23 - The Golden Bowl and the River

Classification: Commentary or later tradition.

Likely sources:

- Jataka Nidana-katha.
- Later Buddha-life retellings.

Notes: The golden bowl floating upstream is a traditional omen before awakening, but it is not a direct sutta episode.

## Scene 24 - Mara's Army Attacks

Classification: Partial sutta.

Likely sources:

- Snp 3.2, Padhana Sutta, for Mara/Namuci approaching the striving Bodhisatta and the list of Mara's armies.
- Jataka Nidana-katha and later art/biography for the dramatic battle imagery, weapons, storms, and flower transformation.

Notes: Mara's opposition is sutta-based. The highly visual battle under the Bodhi tree is mainly later narrative and artistic development.

## Scene 25 - The Temptations of Mara's Daughters

Classification: Direct sutta.

Likely sources:

- SN 4.25, Maradhitu Sutta.

Notes: The daughters of Mara, commonly named Tanha, Arati, and Raga, and their failed attempt to tempt the Buddha are directly sutta-based, though the exact placement in the Buddha-life timeline can vary in retellings.

## Scene 26 - The Earth-Witness and Awakening

Classification: Partial sutta / later biography.

Likely sources:

- MN 36, Mahasaccaka Sutta, for the three knowledges and awakening.
- MN 4, Bhayabherava Sutta, for a parallel account of the three knowledges during the night.
- Jataka Nidana-katha and later Buddha-life traditions for the earth-witness gesture and Mara's challenge over the seat of awakening.

Notes: The awakening itself is directly sutta-based. The earth-touching witness scene is a later biographical and iconographic tradition.

## Scene 27 - Mucalinda Protects the Buddha

Classification: Direct sutta.

Likely sources:

- Ud 2.1, Mucalinda Sutta.
- Vinaya Mahavagga, Mahakhandhaka, for the post-awakening sequence.

Notes: The storm, the naga king coiling around the Buddha, and the Buddha abiding in the bliss of liberation are directly found in canonical material.

## Scene 28 - Brahma Requests the Teaching

Classification: Direct sutta.

Likely sources:

- MN 26, Ariyapariyesana Sutta.
- SN 6.1, Brahmayacana Sutta.
- Vinaya Mahavagga, Mahakhandhaka.

Notes: The Buddha's initial hesitation to teach, Brahma Sahampati's request, and the lotus simile are directly sutta-based.

## Scene 29 - The First Turning of the Wheel

Classification: Direct sutta.

Likely sources:

- SN 56.11, Dhammacakkappavattana Sutta.
- Vinaya Mahavagga, Mahakhandhaka.

Notes: The Middle Way, Four Noble Truths, and Kondanna's arising of the Dhamma eye are directly canonical.

## Scene 30 - The Sangha Gathers Around the Teaching

Classification: Composite.

Likely sources:

- Vinaya Mahavagga, especially the early ordination and missionary narratives.
- Suttas and Vinaya passages about early disciples such as Sariputta and Moggallana.
- Later summary tradition.

Notes: This is not one single story from one sutta. It summarizes the growth of the early Sangha from several canonical and traditional materials.

## Scene 31 - Anathapindika Buys Jetavana

Classification: Vinaya / commentary.

Likely sources:

- Vinaya Cullavagga, Senasanakkhandhaka, for monastery-dwelling regulations and narratives around lodgings.
- Jataka Nidana-katha for the full presentation of Jetavana.
- Commentarial traditions about Anathapindika and Prince Jeta.

Notes: Jetavana appears constantly in sutta settings, but the purchase story with gold covering the ground is mainly Vinaya/commentarial narrative rather than a discourse episode.

## Scene 32 - Yasodhara Meets the Buddha Again

Classification: Commentary or later tradition.

Likely sources:

- Jataka Nidana-katha.
- Later Buddha-life biographies.
- Commentarial traditions connected with Yasodhara/Rahula.

Notes: The suttas preserve Rahula and Kapilavatthu-related materials, but this emotional reunion scene is chiefly later biography.

## Scene 33 - The Buddha Instructs Rahula

Classification: Direct sutta.

Likely sources:

- MN 61, Ambalatthika-rahulovada Sutta.
- Other Rahula teachings such as MN 62 and MN 147 for broader context, though the scene here mainly follows MN 61.

Notes: The teaching on truthfulness and reflecting on bodily, verbal, and mental action as in a mirror is directly sutta-based.

## Scene 34 - Mahapajapati Gotami and the Bhikkhuni Sangha

Classification: Direct sutta / Vinaya.

Likely sources:

- AN 8.51, Gotami Sutta.
- Vinaya Cullavagga X, Bhikkhunikkhandhaka.

Notes: Mahapajapati's request, the involvement of Ananda, and the founding of the bhikkhuni order are preserved in canonical sutta and Vinaya forms. Some details and historical interpretation are debated in modern scholarship.

## Scene 35 - Teaching in Tavatimsa Heaven

Classification: Commentary or later tradition.

Likely sources:

- Dhammapada Commentary / Devorohana Vatthu, the story connected with Dhammapada verse 181.
- Abhidhamma origin traditions.
- Later festival and Buddha-life traditions.

Notes: The story that the Buddha taught his mother in Tavatimsa heaven is central in later Theravada tradition, especially in relation to the Abhidhamma, but it is not a direct early sutta story.

## Scene 36 - The Descent from Heaven

Classification: Commentary or later tradition.

Likely sources:

- Dhammapada Commentary / Devorohana Vatthu, the story connected with Dhammapada verse 181.
- Later Buddha-life traditions.
- Artistic and festival traditions connected with Sankassa.

Notes: The jeweled stairway descent from Tavatimsa is a later biographical and devotional motif rather than a direct Nikaya sutta episode.

## Scene 37 - Angulimala Is Transformed

Classification: Direct sutta.

Likely sources:

- MN 86, Angulimala Sutta.
- Thag 16.8, Angulimalattheragatha, for Angulimala's elder verses.

Notes: The encounter, the line "I have stopped; you stop," Angulimala's going forth, and his later transformation are directly sutta-based.

## Scene 38 - Jivaka Treats the Buddha's Foot

Classification: Vinaya / commentary.

Likely sources:

- SN 1.38, Sakalika Sutta, for the Buddha's foot being pierced by a stone sliver and the Buddha enduring the pain mindfully.
- Vinaya Cullavagga VII, Devadatta-related narratives, for Devadatta's attempt to kill the Buddha with a rock and the wound to the Buddha's foot.
- Dhammapada Commentary / DPPN tradition for Jivaka's treatment details.

Notes: The injury from the rock splinter is preserved in Vinaya. The specific medical treatment scene is mainly later narrative elaboration.

## Scene 39 - The Taming of Nalagiri

Classification: Vinaya.

Likely sources:

- Vinaya Cullavagga VII, Devadatta-related narratives.
- Later retellings and art traditions.

Notes: Devadatta's release of the fierce elephant Nalagiri and the Buddha's calming of the elephant are preserved in Vinaya rather than as a Nikaya sutta.

## Scene 40 - Cunda's Last Offering

Classification: Direct sutta.

Likely sources:

- DN 16, Mahaparinibbana Sutta.

Notes: Cunda's invitation, sukaramaddava, the instruction to bury the remainder, the Buddha's illness, and the instruction to reassure Cunda are directly found in DN 16.

## Scene 41 - The Final Journey to Kusinara

Classification: Direct sutta.

Likely sources:

- DN 16, Mahaparinibbana Sutta.
- DN 17, Mahasudassana Sutta, for the deeper Kusavati/Mahasudassana background.

Notes: The journey after Cunda's meal, the Buddha's weariness, Ananda's concerns about Kusinara, and the Mahasudassana background are canonical.

## Scene 42 - The Parinibbana

Classification: Direct sutta.

Likely sources:

- DN 16, Mahaparinibbana Sutta.

Notes: The twin sala trees, Ananda's grief, the Dhamma and Vinaya as teacher, the final exhortation on heedfulness, and the passing into final Nibbana are directly sutta-based.
